Nyaung Oo Phee Island

Sleep above turquoise lagoons, snorkel thriving reefs, and wake up to sunrise over the Andaman Sea.

Island Overview

Nyaung Oo Phee is a jewel of the southern Mergui Archipelago, known for its glass-clear lagoon, rainbow coral gardens, and floating villas that keep you suspended above an emerald sea. The island remains protected and sustainably developed, limiting daily visitors to preserve its fragile ecosystems.

Spend your days drifting between secluded coves, kayaking over coral heads, or hiking into jungle ridges that spill panoramic views across the Andaman horizon. Nights end with bioluminescent waters shimmering beneath the pier.

Signature Experiences

  • Snorkel the "Coral Garden" reef teeming with clownfish and giant clams.
  • Stay in an overwater villa with sunrise decks and glass-floor sections.
  • Join island-hopping cruises to nearby Boulder Island and Butterfly Bay.
  • Enjoy chef-prepared seafood barbecues right on the sand.

Travel Tips

Pack reef-safe sunscreen, light layers for breezy evenings, and waterproof bags for kayaking adventures. Wi-Fi is limited—embrace digital detox and use resort radios for communication. Bring cash in Myanmar Kyats for eco-fees and gratuities.

Location

Mergui Archipelago, Tanintharyi Region

Coordinates: 10.020° N, 98.444° E

Best Season

November – April (dry skies, calm seas)

Resorts close during monsoon (May – October)

Getting There

  • Flight Yangon → Kawthaung (1h50m)
  • Fast boat Kawthaung → NOP (1h20m)
  • Visa-free for ASEAN; arrange permits via resort

Where to Stay

  • Nyaung Oo Phee Resort – Overwater suites
  • Aureum Island Resort – Beachfront villas
  • Eco Dome Tents – Glamping beneath jungle canopy

Island Highlights

Three unmissable moments that define Nyaung Oo Phee.

Floating Villas

Wake up to coral reefs beneath your deck and endless blue stretching to the horizon.

Coral Gardens

Snorkel in translucent water where sea fans and anemones sway with colorful fish.

Andaman Sunsets

End the day with fiery skies mirrored on the lagoon and the hush of jungle wildlife.
