Cultural & Heritage Sites

Timeless monuments and living traditions across Myanmar.

Heritage Overview

From ancient pagodas to royal palaces, Myanmar’s heritage reflects centuries of craftsmanship, devotion, and cultural exchange, woven into a vibrant tapestry at the crossroads of India, China, and Southeast Asia. Once known as Burma, this land of golden stupas pulses with Theravada Buddhism, where the 99-meter Shwedagon Pagoda in Yangon, encrusted with gems and enshrining relics of four Buddhas, shines as the nation’s holiest site and a canvas of daily devotion. In Bagan, over 2,000 temples from the 9th to 13th centuries, including the grand Ananda and mural-rich Gubyaukgyi, form a stunning medieval cityscape despite earthquake damage. Mandalay’s reconstructed royal palace, with its teak carvings and cosmic layout, and the miraculous Golden Rock of Kyaiktiyo embody faith and wonder.

Living Culture

Traditional arts like lacquerware, woodcarving, and tapestry weaving thrive in workshops and markets across Myanmar. In Bagan, Shan families layer bamboo with glossy black yun lacquer and incise gold leaf designs of mythical creatures. Mandalay’s stone carvers chisel serene Buddhas, while Inle Lake weavers spin lotus threads into delicate scarves. Kengtung parasol makers paint vibrant cotton canopies, and Amarapura bronze casters forge resonant singing bowls. Passed through family apprenticeships, these crafts sustain livelihoods, preserve ancient skills, and keep Myanmar’s cultural spirit alive in every stroke and shine.
