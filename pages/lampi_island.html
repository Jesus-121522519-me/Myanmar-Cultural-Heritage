Lampi Island

Kayak through mangrove mazes, scale limestone ridges, and drift above kaleidoscopic coral.

Island Overview

Iampi Island lies tucked between forested karst peaks and mangrove-draped coves, offering a quieter counterpoint to busier Archipelago stops. Traditional Moken sea gypsies still fish these waters, adding cultural insight to your adventure.

Expect crystal shallows perfect for paddleboards, jungle trails leading to secret lookouts, and night skies sprinkled with unpolluted constellations. Eco lodges limit guest numbers to ensure the reefs stay vibrant and wildlife thrives.

Signature Experiences

  • Kayak through limestone tunnels into hidden blue lagoons.
  • Trek jungle ridges for sunrise panoramas of the archipelago.
  • Snorkel with reef sharks, manta rays, and turtles in protected zones.
  • Learn sustainable fishing techniques from local Moken guides.

Responsible Travel

Respect marine parks by anchoring only at assigned moorings and avoiding coral contact. Bring refillable bottles, carry out all waste, and support community-run lodges that reinvest tourism income locally.

Location

Mergui Archipelago, Tanintharyi Region

Coordinates: 11.050° N, 99.040° E

Best Season

December – April (dry with calm waves)

Green season tours operate on request May – October

Getting There

  • Fly to Kawthaung or Ranong (Thailand)
  • Speedboat transfer (1h45m) arranged by lodge
  • Entry permits handled through registered operators

Where to Stay

  • Iampi Eco Lodge – Solar-powered beachfront bungalows
  • Archipelago Explorer – Boutique liveaboard cruises
  • Moken Homestay – Cultural immersion in stilt villages
