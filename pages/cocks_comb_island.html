Cocks Comb Island

Glide into Myanmar's famous heart-shaped lagoon and uncover sea caves carved by the Andaman Sea.

Island Overview

Cocks Comb Island, also known as Kyun Pila, is an iconic Andaman gem distinguished by its heart-shaped lagoon. Accessible only via a sea arch, the lagoon dazzles with crystal waters, kaleidoscopic sea life, and sheer cliffs blanketed in jungle.

Day tours depart early from Kawthaung or Ranong (Thailand), ensuring you reach the lagoon under perfect light. After snorkelling, relax on sugar-white sandbars or explore nearby islands that are just as untouched.

Signature Experiences

  • Swim through the natural sea arch into the heart lagoon.
  • Snorkel among technicolor corals, angelfish, and reef sharks.
  • Kayak into hidden coves and explore limestone caves.
  • Picnic on secluded beaches with panoramic archipelago views.

Know Before You Go

Only day trips are allowed to protect the marine ecosystem. Follow your guide's briefing for safe lagoon entry, and bring fins to navigate mild currents. Drones require special permission from local authorities.

Location

Mergui Archipelago, Tanintharyi Region

Coordinates: 11.610° N, 98.373° E

Best Season

November – May for calm seas and clear visibility

Limited tours operate in shoulder months (June – July)

Getting There

  • Fly to Kawthaung (Myanmar) or Ranong (Thailand)
  • Join guided speedboat tour (1h30m)
  • Permits and park fees included with registered operators

Pack Essentials

  • Waterproof dry bag, rash guard, snorkel gear
  • Eco-friendly sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Waterproof camera for sea caves and lagoon shots
