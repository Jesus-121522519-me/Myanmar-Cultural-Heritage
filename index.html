About Myanmar

Discover the enchanting blend of natural beauty, ancient heritage and warm hospitality

The Land of Golden Pagodas

Myanmar, formerly known as Burma, is a mystical land located in Southeast Asia. Bordered by China, Thailand, India, Laos and Bangladesh, it's a country blessed with rich biodiversity, abundant natural resources, and a cultural heritage dating back thousands of years.

After decades of isolation, Myanmar has opened its doors to the world, revealing its untouched beauty and preserved traditions. From the shimmering spires of Shwedagon Pagoda to the ancient temples of Bagan, the natural beauty of Inle Lake, and the pristine beaches of Ngapali, Myanmar offers experiences that captivate the heart and inspire the soul.

Culture & Traditions

Immerse yourself in Myanmar's rich cultural heritage and ancient traditions

Buddhism & Spirituality

Buddhism is deeply woven into the fabric of Myanmar's society, influencing daily life, art, and architecture. Monks in their distinctive maroon robes are a common sight, and pagodas dot the landscape as symbols of devotion.

Thanaka

The distinctive yellow-white paste made from ground bark is a unique aspect of Myanmar culture. Applied to the face as both cosmetic and sunscreen, it's a traditional beauty practice dating back centuries.

Traditional Dress

The longyi, a sarong-like garment worn by both men and women, is the traditional attire of Myanmar. Each ethnic group has its own distinctive patterns and styles, reflecting their unique cultural identity.

Festivals & Traditions

Experience Myanmar's vibrant cultural celebrations and time-honored traditions

Thingyan Water Festival

Myanmar's most important festival marking the New Year in April. The entire country celebrates with water throwing, symbolizing the washing away of sins and bad luck from the previous year. Streets come alive with music, dancing, and joyful water fights.

Thadingyut Light Festival

The Festival of Lights celebrates Buddha's return from heaven in October. Millions of candles, oil lamps, and modern lights illuminate pagodas, homes, and streets across Myanmar, creating a magical atmosphere of spiritual devotion and community celebration.

Waso Festival

Marking the beginning of Buddhist Lent in July, this festival sees monks retreat for three months of intensive meditation and study. It's a time for spiritual reflection and merit-making through donations and acts of kindness.

Kathina Festival

Celebrating the end of Buddhist Lent in October/November, communities come together to offer new robes and necessities to monks. It's one of the most merit-making occasions in the Buddhist calendar, strengthening community bonds.
